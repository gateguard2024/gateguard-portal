# MSO Prospect Meeting — Talk Track
**Total time: 35–40 minutes**
*This is your guide, not a script. Know the flow, not the words.*

---

## Before You Start (1 min)

Set the tone. Don't launch into the deck right away.

> *"Before I show you anything — tell me a little about where you are today. How are you currently making money in this space, and what made you want to look at something like this?"*

Let them talk for 2–3 minutes. You're listening for:
- What kind of properties they already touch
- Whether they have existing dealer relationships
- What their frustration is with the current model
- How big they're thinking

This shapes which parts of the deck you emphasize.

---

## Slide 1 — Title (30 sec)

Just land the headline.

> *"What I want to show you today is how GateGuard built a model where you're not just a dealer — you're the person that other dealers plug into. You own the network above them, and you earn on everything they do. That's the MSO opportunity."*

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem (3 min)

Don't rush this. If they're in the space, they already feel this pain.

> *"You've probably seen this firsthand — properties have a gate from one company, cameras from another, access control from a third, and nobody talks to each other. The property manager doesn't know who to call. The dealer who installed it is gone. There's no recurring relationship, no recurring revenue."*

Pause. Ask:
> *"Does that sound familiar to you?"*

Let them validate it. Then:
> *"That's the gap we built GateGuard to fill. One company, one platform, one relationship — and it creates recurring revenue for everyone in the chain."*

---

## Slide 3 — The Full Stack (3–4 min)

Three columns. Walk left to right.

**Hardware:**
> *"We make our own equipment — gate operators, barriers, cameras, intercoms, access panels. All of it. So you're not patching together five different brands on every job."*

**Software (NEXUS):**
> *"This is what makes us different from every other hardware company. Every dealer in your network runs their entire operation through our portal — quoting, work orders, billing, scheduling, everything. You have full visibility into what every dealer in your network is doing."*

**AI Army:**
> *"This is where it gets really interesting. We built eight AI agents directly into the platform. ARIA can research any multifamily property and give you a full intelligence briefing — the decision maker's name, email format, what systems they have, when their contracts expire — in under 60 seconds. TRINITY handles inbound calls. FORGE builds quotes. None of your competition has this."*

---

## Slide 4 — NEXUS Portal (2–3 min)

Keep this brief. Reference it, don't demo it here.

> *"I'll show you a live demo after this — but the six modules your dealers use every day are: their sales pipeline and quotes, work order dispatch, their entire dealer network management, lead intelligence with ARIA, billing, and the calendar. Everything in one login."*

> *"For you as an MSO, you log in and you see all of it. Every dealer, every job, every open quote in your network."*

---

## Slide 5 — The Hierarchy (4 min)

This is the emotional center of the meeting. Take your time.

> *"Here's where you sit."* [Point to the highlighted MSO row]

> *"GateGuard sits at the top — we make the product, run the platform, handle support. Below us, you're the MSO. You own a geographic territory or a vertical. Below you, you have Full Dealers — they're the ones selling and installing at properties. Below them are the properties themselves."*

> *"The key thing to understand is this: you earn on everything below you. Not just your own sales. Every piece of hardware every dealer in your network sells. Every monthly software fee on every property they manage. That override compounds every single month."*

Pause.
> *"The question I always ask people is — would you rather own one store, or own the block the stores are on?"*

---

## Slide 6 — Three Revenue Streams (5 min)

Go slow here. This is the money slide.

**Stream 01 — Hardware:**
> *"When a dealer in your network buys equipment, they're buying at dealer pricing. But you're getting MSO pricing — which is a better discount. The spread between what you pay and what they pay is your hardware margin. Every gate, every camera, every reader."*

**Stream 02 — MRR Override:**
> *"This is the one that changes everything. Every property in your network pays a monthly software fee. You get a per-unit override on that — not a percentage, a per-unit amount — every month, as long as they're active. It starts small. But it compounds. 500 units is $250 a month. 5,000 units is $2,500. 50,000 units is life-changing money that shows up whether you made a sale that month or not."*

**Stream 03 — Dealer Network:**
> *"And the third stream is simply — the more dealers you recruit and activate, the wider your base. You're not just selling. You're building infrastructure."*

---

## Slide 7 — Scale Math (4–5 min)

Make this feel real and achievable, not abstract.

> *"Let me show you what this looks like in practice with conservative numbers — 20 dealers, 5 properties each, 80 units per property. That's 8,000 active units."*

> *"8,000 units at $0.50 a unit is $4,000 a month — $48,000 a year — just from the software override. Before you've sold a single piece of hardware."*

> *"Then you add hardware. If those 20 dealers each did one install a year at a $6,000 average — that's another $120,000."*

> *"Year one, conservative, is over $168,000. And here's the thing — the MRR keeps growing every month. It doesn't reset."*

Then ask:
> *"How many dealers do you think you could realistically recruit in your territory in year one?"*

Let them do the math in their head. It's more powerful than you telling them.

## Slide 8 — The AI Advantage (3 min)

Don't oversell this. Let the capability speak.

> *"I want to spend just a minute on this because it's what your dealers get that no other platform gives them."*

> *"ARIA — in the time it would take a rep to Google a property and maybe find a phone number — ARIA gives you the decision maker's name, their title, their email format, what gate system they currently have, what ISP they're on, when their contract expires, and a recommended pitch strategy. In under 60 seconds."*

> *"FORGE means a brand new rep can build a professional, margin-checked quote in 5 minutes — with all the right parts, all the right dependencies flagged, and a client-facing branded proposal ready to send."*

> *"You're giving every dealer in your network the tools that used to only exist inside a very sophisticated operation. That's what makes good dealers great, and what makes great dealers dominant."*

---

## Slide 9 — Why Now (2–3 min)

Quick, confident, factual.

> *"A couple of reasons this moment matters. First — millions of bulk access and gate contracts signed between 2018 and 2022 are expiring right now, through 2028. Properties are actively shopping. That window is open."*

> *"Second — new construction. 250,000 multifamily units came online in 2024 alone. Most of them are still making decisions on systems."*

> *"And third — your competition. DoorKing, LiftMaster, Linear — these are great hardware brands but they're analog-era. No portal. No AI. No recurring revenue model for the dealer. The territory that gets an AI-powered MSO first owns that market for years."*

---

## Slide 10 — Next Steps (2–3 min)

Be direct. Don't leave it vague.

> *"Three steps to get going. First — we send you the MSO agreement. It comes through our portal as an e-signature, you can review and sign same week. Second — once that's signed, your account is activated in NEXUS within 48 hours. Full access, all AI tools, everything. Third — we start working with you on dealer recruitment. GateGuard provides the training, the onboarding wizards, the proposal templates — you bring the relationships."*

> *"We've activated MSOs in under two weeks start to finish."*

Then close:
> *"Based on what you've told me about your territory and the dealers you know — does this feel like the right time to move?"*

---

## If They Ask About Exclusivity

> *"MSO agreements are territory-based. We work with you to define your territory before signing. If you're the first MSO in that market, you have a significant first-mover advantage — we're not out there trying to fill territories with five competing MSOs."*

## If They Ask About Startup Cost

> *"The MSO agreement itself doesn't require a large upfront investment. What you're investing is time to recruit and activate dealers. The hardware discounts and MRR override start earning from day one of your first activated property."*

## If They Ask About Training

> *"Full onboarding in NEXUS. Your dealers get their own training modules inside the platform. GateGuard walks every new dealer through the portal. You're not doing tech support — you're doing relationship management."*

## If They Want to See the Platform Live

> *"I can pull it up right now. Let me show you what an ARIA search looks like — type in any multifamily property name in your market and we'll see what it knows."*

# Intern A — 12-Hour Work Plan
## You OWN the End-User Experience: Lead → Customer

Your job for 2 days (~6 hrs/day): be the office user. Take a brand-new lead all the way to a finished, invoiced customer — and own finding every bug and every spot that isn't **easy enough for a 5th grader**. Test on **beta**.

Use the step list in `INTERN1_END_USER_TEST.md` as your click-by-click script. This plan is how to spend the 12 hours and what to hand back.

**One question to score on every screen:** "Would a 5th grader know what to do here without asking?"
**Keep two running lists the whole time:** 🐞 Bugs (broken/error/nothing happens) and 😕 Confusing (works, but unclear).

---

## DAY 1 (6 hours)

**Hour 1 — Orientation & setup**
- Log in to beta. Tour the bottom tabs (My Day, Sales, Jobs, Operations, Design, Systems, Money/Docs) and the "Ask Nexus anything" bar.
- Read the "coming soon — don't log as a bug" list in `INTERN1_END_USER_TEST.md`. Have a test email ready (to play the customer).

**Hours 2–3 — Run the full happy path once, slowly, logging every step**
Lead → work the lead (note/call/follow-up/status/attachment) → Create Opportunity → Quote/Proposal → send → (be the customer) approve → Mark Won → Create Project (job) → Dispatch a tech → Mark complete → Invoice → find the Customer/Site.
- For EVERY step write: did it work? was it obvious? how many seconds to figure out?

**Hour 4 — Ease-of-use scoring pass**
- Go screen by screen. Score each 1–5 for "5th-grader simple." Note any word a normal person wouldn't know (MRR? stage? proposal vs agreement?). Note any text that overlaps, is too dim, or too small.

**Hour 5 — Break-it pass**
- Try to break each step: empty fields, weird characters, double-click, hit Back mid-flow, refresh mid-task, click every button. Note anything that errors, does nothing, or loses your data.
- Check desktop vs phone size: does anything overlap or hide behind the bottom bar?

**Hour 6 — Day 1 report**
- Write up: top 5 bugs, top 5 confusing spots, and the 3 screens that felt best.

---

## DAY 2 (6 hours)

**Hour 7 — Run it again with a different story**
- New scenario: a walk-in lead, a multi-line quote, and this time the customer **Requests Changes / Asks a Question** instead of approving. Does the revise→resend loop make sense?

**Hour 8 — Edge cases**
- Duplicate lead (same name) — does it warn you? A quote with no customer email. A very large quote. Reassigning a lead. Marking a job complete with nothing filled in.

**Hour 9 — Devices**
- Repeat the core flow on a phone and an iPad and a desktop. Note where the layout feels cramped (phone), or too empty/centered (desktop). Flag overlaps.

**Hour 10 — Does the data stick?**
- Refresh and reopen each record (lead, opportunity, job, invoice, customer). Did your notes/changes save? Does the Customer/Site record actually show the job + invoice?

**Hour 11 — Rank everything**
- Combine both days into ONE ranked list: top 10 issues, each marked 🐞 bug or 😕 confusing, with where it happened and what you expected.

**Hour 12 — Final report + walkthrough**
- 1-page summary: "Can a brand-new person go from a phone lead to a paid, installed customer without help? Where do they get stuck?" Be ready to screen-share the flow.

---

## What to hand back
- The ranked top-10 list (bug vs confusing, screen, expected vs actual, screenshot).
- Per-screen 5th-grader scores.
- One sentence: the single biggest thing that would make this flow easier.
